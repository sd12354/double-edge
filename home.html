A new report from Redfin looks at home values three years following a wild fire. It found that in areas directly hit by a fire, home values increased 21%, but in areas outside of the fire zone, housing prices increased 33% during the same period.

In Conclusion

In conclusion, the data presented underscores the significant impact of wildfires on housing markets and insurance industries in California. Properties located within fire perimeters experienced varied price changes, with those closer to the fire seeing both declines and appreciations in value. The withdrawal or reduction of coverage by major insurance companies in wildfire-prone areas further highlights the challenges faced by homeowners in obtaining protection. The decline in the construction of homes in high-risk fire zones and the increased costs associated with building wildfire-resistant structures illustrate the ongoing efforts to mitigate wildfire risks.
